About Ithraa

Encouraging talent, inspiring minds

Ithra offers to its new visitors inspiring experiences that nurture creativity and a passion for learning. It is a beacon of change and a window to global experiences by encouraging creativity and inspiring minds. The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia is keen to follow this development in multiple ways. Saudi Aramco presented this project as it worked to enrich a fertile environment for innovation and experiment by stimulating talent through sponsoring arts, literature and sciences.

Ithraa contains a number of different sections, which are:

  • Idea Lab
  • The library and the theatre
  • Cinema
  • The Museum
  • Children's Museum
  • Energy Exhibition
  • The Great Hall
  • Ithra Tower

Ithra Basics:

  1. Creativity, culture and knowledge.

    2. It is a new and unlimited source of knowledge and creativity, in order to satisfy the curiosity of minds wishing to learn and innovate.

  2. Mission and vision:

    3. Raising the passion for knowledge, creativity and communication across cultures for a prosperous future for the Kingdom, creating a future in which the Kingdom will be a beacon of knowledge and creativity.

  3. History:

    4. Ithraa Center is located in the same spot where the first oil well was discovered in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia in the city of Dhahran, known as (Bir al-Khair). It started work in the period from 15 Jumada Al-Awwal 1429 AH corresponding to May 20, 2008 AD. The foundation stone of the project was laid by King Abdullah bin Abdulaziz Al Saud and it was opened in 2018.

  4. Building structure:

    5. Ithraa Center received the highest category of “Excellence” award from the American Concrete Institute (ACI) at the Concrete Construction Awards Ceremony in October 2019. The building and its facilities cover an area of 80,000 square meters. The impressive building includes a 4-storey library, a tower The 18-storey Ithra, the 3-storey Idea Lab, the Energy Gallery, the 5-gallery Museum, the 315-seat cinema, the 900-seat performing arts theatre, the 1,500-square-meter Great Hall, and the Children's Museum.



  5. Building Elements:

    6. The building structure consists of stainless steel pipes that work to resist fire and sound insulation using compressed natural materials of sand, gravel and clay, where the building elements are committed to be in accordance with international standards and well-known standards (LEED) approved with gold grade, where their use is highly efficient and saves energy for many years .
